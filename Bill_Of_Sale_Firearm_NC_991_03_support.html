Basic Rules of Useage

When Using:
  1. The Buyer and Seller MUST agree otherwise the Buyer/Seller signature of affirmation MUST not be afixed to this document.
  2. NC Diver licenses (NCDL), NC firearm permits/licenses CCH, FFL retailer receipts related to NICS transactions/log entries, etc. must be copied/photographed. Then they must be attached to the Bill-Of-Sale.
  3. (Recommended) Both the Buyer and Seller should initialize each pageof the Bill Of Sale and any pin/ink changes/corrections.
  4. (Recommended) In the event an error or something need to be clarified after the Buyer and Seller have spearated with a signed signature of affirmation, the discoverer should notify the second party of the observation and communicate action taken to reconcile. Anything discovered error or anomaly beyond an correctable oversight or typographical error should be communicate, addressed, and documented for record on the Bill Of Sale each party retained. Example: Buyer's license has date of birth (DOB) but the Seller's copy of the Bill-Of-Sale does not reflect that information. However, the Seller's copy of the Bill-Of-Sale has the Buy's NCDL. That artifact reflects the appripriate DOB of the Buyer. The Seller should note the correction and notify the Buyer of addition.
  5. Both the Buyer and Seller must each have a copy upon "walk-away" from this transaction.

      (Recommended)Either tthe Buyer or Seller must play the role of "recorder".
      • The "Recorder" must collect the electronic copies of the Bill-Of-Sale_For_Firearm In North Carolina as well as attached artifacts.
      • The "Recorder's" version becomes the primary, parent artifact of the transaction.
    • The Buyer or Seller who is not the "Recorder" must serve in the "auditor" role.
      • The "auditor" double checks instances where the buyer and seller must agree.
      • The "auditor" insures that the electronic copies are the same filesize (bytes) and contain the same content (buyer/seller inputs) by reading the *.PDF version.
      • The "auditor" insures both Buyer and Seller have complete set of copies prior to "walk-away".
