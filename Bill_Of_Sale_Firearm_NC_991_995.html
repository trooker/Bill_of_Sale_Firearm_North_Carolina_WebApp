Bearing Arms in North Carolina

Disclaimers are listed at the end of this document. The Buyer and Seller should read these disclaimers before proceeding. Just scrolling to the Disclaimers section should convey to the Buyer and Seller the complexity involved in the responsibilities placed on the participants of a legal firearm sale in North Carolina. North Carolina legislated these responsibilities to keep its citizens safe and to maintain a well organized militia. This document seeks to assist the Buyer and Seller in the performance of their responsibilities.



Intended Use

The intended use of this "work" is to provide an artifact that can accurately describe the sales transaction between two private North Carolina citizens. The term "work" includes the associated html script, javascript modules, style sheets, images, blank pdf versions of the bill-of-sales, etc. which reside under configuration control of Abbott Analytical Products (AAP) . Noncommercial replication is allowed of any and all of the "work's" components/modules. Appropriate attribution must appear in all "derivative work" employing any of AAP's intellectual property.

Modes of Use

This Bill-Of-Sale_For_Firearm In North Carolina form may be used in any of the following modes:

  1. Mode 1: A simple hardcopy of a firearm bill-of-sale: Buyer/Seller click the the hot link to the "What to Do Next Checklist" section. Print a hardcopy following Step 7. Manually complete and sign the form. Then assemble ancillary documents and continue with the transaction which th form documents. Buyer/Seller must both leave with a copy of the Bill-of-Sale and all ancillary documents.
  2. Mode 2: The Buyer/Seller co-locate to complete the bill-of-sale from the top to the "Signature Block". Then they print a hardcopy, review the prior electronic inputs for correctness, indicated satisfaction with their signature in the appropriate block. Then they continue the transaction as described in Mode 1.
  3. Mode 3: The Buyer/Seller co-locate to complete the bill-of-sale, indicate their satisfaction with the signature, print a hardcopy of the PDF version, assemble ancillary documents, ontinue the transaction as described in Mode 1
  4. Mode 4: The Buyer/Seller participate as described in Mode 3 plus send the entire collection of Bill-Of-Sale_For_Firearm In North Carolina and ancillary documents via email to selected addresses.

Basic Rules of Useage

When Using:
  1. The Buyer and Seller MUST agree otherwise the Buyer/Seller signature of affirmation MUST not be afixed to this document.
  2. NC Diver licenses, NC firearm permits/licenses must be copied. Then they must become a part of the document.
  3. Both the Buyer and Seller must each have a copy upon "walk-away" from this transaction.
  4. Either tthe Buyer or Seller must play the role of "recorder".
    • The "Recorder" must collect the electronic copies of the Bill-Of-Sale_For_Firearm In North Carolina as well as attached artifacts.
    • The "Recorder's" version becomes the primary, parent artifact of the transaction.
  5. The Buyer or Seller who is not the "Recorder" must serve in the "auditor" role.
    • The "auditor" double checks instances where the buyer and seller must agree.
    • The "auditor" insures that the electronic copies are the same filesize (bytes) and contain the same content (buyer/seller inputs) by reading the *.PDF version.
    • The "auditor" insures both Buyer and Seller have complete set of copies prior to "walk-away".
